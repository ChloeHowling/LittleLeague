Who we are

We are the Social Impact League! A community formed around solving real world challenges together! Teams of up to four students work together on a project centered on one of six social impact pathways - direct service (green), community engaged learning & research (blue), social entreprenuership & corporate social responsibility (purple), policy & governance (red), community organizing & activism (orange), and philanthropy (yellow). You can learn more about each topic on the official UVU Office for social impact website.





What we do

Over the course of 3 months, teams have the option to work together on one of the following three types of projects:

  • Prototype Sprint

    students identify a problem that they are passionate about and then use (or acquire) their skills needed to develop and present an early technical/organizational prototype that solves a need for a population or community; these projects are similar to those in the Microsoft Imagine Cup.

  • Campaign Development

    Students identify a public issue through polling and utilize their marketing skills to develop a strategic plan for a public call-to-action or educational goal. Campaigns are encouraged to collaborate with companies, not-for-profits, government entities, and other partners. For more examples, visit the article on Social Impact Campaign by azure media.

  • Workshop Development

    Students create content that fellow students and Social Impact fellows can use to teach their communities.


Student team members may receive the following benefits as a result of participation:

Scholarship
up to full tuition per school year.
Mentorship
valuable feedback from social impact ambassadors, technical professionals, and resume advice.
Recognition
project showcase to students, faculty, alumni, and national and international impact organizations.





How to join

Send an email to thisisademo@uvu.edu before 12/15/2022 with the following information:

  • All team members' name and student ID
  • The team name
  • Up to two of the six pathways chosen
  • Description of project
